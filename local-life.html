Local Life

More than just a place to visit — Newark-on-Trent is a thriving community with a strong sense of identity and a wonderful quality of life.

A Community That Cares

Newark is the kind of place where people look out for each other. With a population of around 42,000, it's large enough to have everything you need but small enough that you'll recognise faces at the market. Newcomers often remark on how quickly they feel part of the community.

The town benefits from excellent transport links — London is less than an hour and a half away by train, yet property prices remain considerably more affordable than the south-east. It's an increasingly popular choice for families, professionals who commute, and those seeking a better quality of life without sacrificing connectivity.

Schools & Education

Newark has a good range of schools catering to all ages and needs.

Primary Schools

Newark and the surrounding area are served by several primary schools, including Church of England, Catholic, and non-denominational options. Many are rated Good or Outstanding by Ofsted and benefit from strong community involvement.

Secondary & Further Education

The Magnus Church of England Academy continues a tradition of education dating back to 1530. Newark Academy and other secondary schools serve the town well. Newark College provides further education and vocational training for post-16 students.

Shopping

From the historic Market Square to independent boutiques, Newark has a shopping scene to suit every taste.

Newark Market

One of the largest traditional markets in the East Midlands. Fresh produce, clothing, household goods, and street food spread across the magnificent cobbled Market Square on Monday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday and Saturday.

The Buttermarket

A covered shopping area in the town centre housing a mix of independent retailers and small businesses. From vintage clothing to artisan food, the Buttermarket is a hub for independent traders.

High Street & Beyond

Newark's high street blends familiar names with independent shops. Antiques dealers, bookshops, craft suppliers, and specialist food shops give the town centre its distinctive character. The antiques and collectables quarter along Castlegate is a treasure trove.

Food & Drink

Newark punches above its weight when it comes to eating and drinking. The town has an impressive range of restaurants, gastropubs, cafes, and takeaways to suit all tastes and budgets.

The Market Square is ringed by historic pubs — the White Hart and the Clinton Arms among them — while the Castle Barge offers the unique experience of dining on a floating pub on the River Trent. Independent coffee shops have flourished in recent years, and the market itself offers excellent street food on trading days.

Newark also has a proud brewing heritage. The town was once home to several breweries, and real ale remains popular in the town's many traditional pubs.

Sports, Leisure & Green Spaces

An active lifestyle is easy to maintain in Newark, with excellent facilities and beautiful surroundings.

Sports & Fitness

  • Newark Sports & Fitness Centre — Swimming pool, gym, fitness classes, and sports halls
  • Newark Town FC — Local non-league football club with a proud following
  • Newark Rugby Club — Active rugby union club welcoming players of all abilities
  • Newark Cricket Club — One of the oldest cricket clubs in the area
  • Tennis, bowls, and athletics clubs are also active in the town

Parks & Green Spaces

  • Sconce & Devon Park — Large park with the Civil War earthwork, play areas, cafe, and woodland walks
  • Castle Gardens — Beautifully maintained gardens by the castle overlooking the river
  • Riverside walks — Scenic paths along the River Trent, ideal for walking and cycling
  • Balderton Lakes — A peaceful spot for fishing, walking, and wildlife watching just south of town
  • The surrounding Nottinghamshire countryside offers miles of footpaths and bridleways

Community Groups & Organisations

Getting involved in community life is easy in Newark. There are groups and organisations for almost every interest.

Arts & Culture

Newark has a lively arts scene, including amateur dramatic societies, art groups, photography clubs, and the Palace Theatre's community programme. The town's creative community regularly stages exhibitions and events.

Volunteering

From heritage conservation at the castle to supporting local charities, there are many ways to volunteer and make a difference. The Community & Voluntary sector in Newark is strong and welcoming.

Social & Special Interest

Book clubs, gardening societies, walking groups, history societies, and many more bring people together around shared interests. The town's community centres host regular activities for all ages.

Newark-on-Trent

A historic market town on the River Trent in Nottinghamshire, steeped in civil war history and surrounded by beautiful countryside. This community website aims to be your go-to guide for everything Newark.
