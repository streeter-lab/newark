Things To Do

From medieval castle ruins to Europe's biggest antiques fair, Newark-on-Trent has something for every visitor.

Heritage

Newark Castle & Gardens

Explore the dramatic ruins of this 12th-century castle on the banks of the River Trent. Free entry to the grounds and gardens, with guided tours of the towers and underground passages available.

Open daily, dawn to dusk
Heritage

National Civil War Centre

The UK's only museum dedicated to telling the story of the English Civil War. Interactive exhibits, artefacts, and the Nottinghamshire Civil War Trail bring this turbulent chapter of history to life.

Tue–Sun, 10am–5pm
Heritage

Newark Air Museum

A wide collection of aircraft including a Vulcan bomber and Buccaneer, plus engines, cockpits, and aviation memorabilia. Regular open cockpit events let visitors climb inside historic planes.

Open daily, 10am–5pm (summer)
Shopping

Newark Market

One of the largest traditional markets in the East Midlands, held on the magnificent cobbled Market Square. Fresh produce, clothing, household goods, street food, and more.

Mon, Wed, Thu, Fri & Sat
Culture

The Palace Theatre

Newark's much-loved theatre and entertainment venue, hosting a varied programme of drama, comedy, music, pantomime, and cinema throughout the year.

See website for show times
Outdoors

Queen's Sconce & Sconce Hills Park

A remarkable Civil War star-shaped earthwork fortification set within a beautiful park. Woodland paths, play areas, open fields, and panoramic views from the top of the sconce mound.

Open daily, free entry
Outdoors

River Trent Walks

Follow the scenic riverside path from the castle grounds along the Trent. Enjoy peaceful views, spot local wildlife, watch narrowboats navigate the locks, and discover riverside pubs along the way.

Always accessible
Shopping

Newark Antiques & Collectors Fair

One of Europe's largest antiques events, held at Newark Showground with over 2,000 stalls. Furniture, jewellery, art, ceramics, and curiosities from across the continent.

Several times per year
Food & Drink

The Castle Barge

Newark's famous floating pub, a converted grain barge moored on the River Trent with views of the castle. A unique spot for a drink or meal in characterful surroundings.

Open daily

Nearby Attractions

Newark is perfectly placed for exploring some of the East Midlands' finest destinations.

Sherwood Forest

The legendary home of Robin Hood is just a short drive north. Walk among ancient oaks, visit the Major Oak, and explore the visitor centre. Perfect for a family day out.

Lincoln Cathedral

One of the finest Gothic cathedrals in Europe, Lincoln is around 30 minutes from Newark by car or train. The medieval quarter and castle are well worth a visit too.

Southwell Minster

This stunning Norman minster, known for its remarkable Chapter House carvings, is just 15 minutes from Newark. The charming town of Southwell is delightful to explore.

Newark-on-Trent

A historic market town on the River Trent in Nottinghamshire, steeped in civil war history and surrounded by beautiful countryside. This community website aims to be your go-to guide for everything Newark.
