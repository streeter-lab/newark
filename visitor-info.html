Visitor Information

Everything you need to plan a visit to Newark-on-Trent. Getting here is easy, and you'll find a warm welcome when you arrive.

Getting Here

Newark is well connected by road and rail, making it easy to reach from across the country.

By Train

Newark has two railway stations:

  • Newark North Gate — on the East Coast Main Line (LNER, Grand Central). Direct services from London King's Cross in approximately 1 hour 20 minutes. Also served by trains to/from Edinburgh, York, Leeds, and Peterborough.
  • Newark Castle — on the Nottingham to Lincoln line (East Midlands Railway). Convenient for reaching Newark from Nottingham (~25 mins) and Lincoln (~20 mins).

By Car

Newark sits at a junction of major routes:

  • A1 — the Great North Road runs just east of the town, providing fast access from London (approx. 2.5 hours) and the north.
  • A46 — connecting Newark to Leicester and the M1 to the south-west, and Lincoln to the north-east.
  • A17 — links Newark to Sleaford and the Lincolnshire coast.

East Midlands Airport is around an hour's drive away.

By Bus

Regular bus services connect Newark to surrounding towns and cities:

  • Services to and from Nottingham, Lincoln, Grantham, and Sleaford
  • The bus station is centrally located on Lombard Street, a short walk from the Market Square
  • Stagecoach and other operators run frequent services throughout the day

Parking

There's ample parking available in and around the town centre.

Town Centre Car Parks

  • London Road — Large car park close to the castle and town centre. Short and long-stay options available.
  • Riverside — Near the River Trent, convenient for castle and riverside walks.
  • Appletongate — Central location with easy access to the Market Square.
  • Castle House — Council car park, centrally located.

All car parks are clearly signposted with reasonable fees. Many offer pay-and-display or pay-by-phone options.

Tips for Visitors

  • On market days (Mon, Wed, Thu, Fri, Sat), town centre parking can be busier — arrive early or use the larger car parks.
  • On-street parking is available on some roads with time restrictions — check signs carefully.
  • Both railway stations have their own car parks if you're combining rail and road travel.
  • The town centre is very walkable — most attractions are within 10 minutes' walk of the Market Square.

Where to Stay

From comfortable hotels to cosy B&Bs, Newark has a range of accommodation to suit every budget.

Hotels

Several hotels are located in and around Newark, from historic coaching inns on the Market Square to modern chain hotels near the A1. The White Hart Hotel, dating from the 14th century, is a notable option right in the town centre.

Bed & Breakfasts

For a more personal stay, Newark and the surrounding villages offer a selection of welcoming B&Bs and guest houses. Many are set in period properties and provide a great base for exploring the area.

Holiday Lets & Camping

Self-catering cottages and apartments are available in the town and surrounding countryside. For those who prefer the outdoors, caravan and camping sites can be found in the nearby Nottinghamshire countryside.

Tourist Information

For visitor enquiries, the National Civil War Centre on Appleton Gate serves as an information point. The friendly staff can help with directions, attraction information, and local tips.

For more information, visit the Newark & Sherwood District Council website.
